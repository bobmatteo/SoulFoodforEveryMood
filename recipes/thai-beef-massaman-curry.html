Thai Beef Massaman Curry

Preparation

45 min

Cook

60 min

Spicy

Not spicy

Serves

4

Ingredients

800g Chuck Beef
3-4 Potatoes, cubed
4 tbsp Rapeseed or Sunflower Oil
1 Red Onion, cut in 8 pieces, seperated in layers
70g Roasted Peanuts
4 tbsp Storebought Massaman Curry, preferably from an asian supermarket
400ml Coconut Milk
1 tbsp Palm Sugar
1 tsp Tamarid Paste
3 tbsp Thai Fish Sauce
Salt to taste
Cilianto, for garnish
Red chili, sliced, for garnish

Thai Beef Massaman Curry

  1. Put a cast iron pan (with lit) on the stove. Heat 2 tbsp of oil and add the chuck beef to the pan. Fry the beef until golden brown on both sides.
  2. Poor just enough water over the beef to be fully covered. Let the meat simmer with a covered lit for 2,5-3 hours. The meat is ready when it starts to fall apart. Shred the meat with two forks.
  3. Make sure to save the broth when taking out the meat. You will need to use this later on.
  4. Boil the potatoes in a separate pan until just cooked. Take of the heat and drain.
  5. Heat a large wok or frying pan. Heat 2 tbsp of oil and the peanuts and onion. Fry it for about 3 minutes.
  6. Add 4 tbsp of Massaman Curry paste. Keep un stirring until it gets fragant. This will take around 2-3 minutes.
  7. Now add the coconut milk, palm suger, fish sauce, tamarind paste, 250ml of broth, the beef and potatoes to your wok or frying pan. Let the curry simmer for about 15 minutes. Add salt taste. If the curry gets to thick, you can add some more broth.
  8. Take your Beef Massaman Curry of the stove. Garnish it with ciliantro and red chili. Best served with steamed white rice.
